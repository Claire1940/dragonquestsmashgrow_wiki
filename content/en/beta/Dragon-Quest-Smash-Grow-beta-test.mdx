## Character Customization and the Initial Tutorial

Upon launching the game, players are greeted with a robust customization suite. You can choose between male and female protagonists, with further options to adjust eye shapes, skin tones, hair styles, and voices. Many fans have noted that the hair options allow for a "Super Saiyan" aesthetic, fitting the legendary art style associated with the series.

The tutorial drops you into a mysterious forest where you meet a chatty machine named Roly. This sequence introduces the basic movement and combat controls. Unlike traditional turn-based entries, this title utilizes an automatic attack system. Your character swings their weapon whenever they are stationary near an enemy, with every third strike dealing massive "heavy" damage.

## Mastering the Roguelite Loop: Blessings and Rifts

The core of the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow beta test** gameplay revolves around entering "Rifts." These are procedurally generated stages where the environment and enemy placements shift with every foray. To progress, you must defeat waves of monsters and collect growth crystals to level up mid-run.

Each level-up grants a "Blessing," which acts as a passive or active power-up for that specific run. These blessings are the key to handling larger groups of enemies and the "Alpha" bosses found at the end of each Rift.

### Common Blessing Types

| Blessing Name | Effect Description | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| **Sword of Damocles** | Final normal attack drops a massive sword from the sky. | Best for **Single Target DPS** against Alphas. |
| **Magic Spheres** | Orbits your character, damaging nearby foes. | Excellent for **Defensive Play** and kiting. |
| **Magic Projectiles** | Fires auto-targeting bolts at random enemies. | Perfect for **Chip Damage** while moving. |
| **Attack Speed Aura** | Increases the frequency of all normal attacks. | Synergizes with the **Third Strike** mechanic. |

## The Transmuter: Gacha and Gear Systems

Once you complete the initial tutorial and reach Lunia Village, you gain access to the Transmuter. This is the game's gacha system, where you spend Gems to acquire new weapons and accessories. During the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow beta test**, players are given an "Infinite Reroll" voucher, allowing them to cycle through 3-star weapons until they find one that suits their playstyle.

The current pull rate for 3-star (Legendary) items is approximately 7%, which is relatively generous for a mobile title. It is important to note that the gacha currently focuses on gear rather than characters, meaning your progression is tied to the rarity and enhancement level of your equipment.

### Notable 3-Star Weapons

*   **Falcon’s Favorite (Sword):** Increases movement speed and grants immunity to certain movement-restricting debuffs. Its "Cudigra" (special skill) is a rapid-fire wind slash.
*   **Fire Blade:** Launches explosive fire projectiles whenever an enemy is defeated. Ideal for clearing large clusters of Slimes.
*   **Flashing Talons (Claws):** Transforms standard attacks into lightning-infused blades, offering the highest attack speed in the current beta build.
*   **Gleaming Whip:** Enhances the Sword of Damocles blessing with light-infused follow-up attacks.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not delete the game app during the closed beta period. Your account data is tied to the specific installation, and you may lose access to your beta progress and rewards.

## Progression and the Adventure Log S

Your long-term goals are tracked through the "Adventure Log S," a magical tome provided by the goddess. This log serves as your primary quest hub and rank tracker. By clearing stages and performing "Feats" (daily and weekly missions), you earn experience to increase your Adventure Rank.

Raising your rank is vital because it unlocks new features, such as the "Slime Invasion" event quests and advanced gear enhancement slots. The beta also includes a "Feat Sheet"—a bingo-style reward board that grants up to 10,000 Gems for completing specific milestones like defeating the Alpha Golem or performing a set number of Transmutations.

## Combat Strategies for Alpha Bosses

The "Alpha" monsters are significantly larger and more dangerous than standard field mobs. In the first chapter, you will encounter the Troll and eventually the Alpha Golem. Unlike small fry, Alphas have telegraphed attacks that require precise positioning.

1.  **Kiting with Projectiles:** If you have the Magic Projectile or Piercing Arrow blessings, you can stay outside the boss's melee range. The projectiles will continue to fire even while you are moving to avoid ground slams.
2.  **Cudigra Management:** Your "Cudigra" meter fills as you deal damage. Save this ultimate ability for when the boss is stationary or when you need a brief moment of invincibility during the animation.
3.  **Status Ailment Synergy:** Some weapons, like the Dark Sword of Damocles, deal bonus damage if the enemy is affected by a status ailment. Pair these with blessings that cause "Freeze" or "Spell Lock" for maximum efficiency.
