## Combat Mechanics and Character Classes

Combat in Dragon Quest Smash Grow is about more than just moving around. You can field up to three characters at once, swapping between them instantly to adapt to the battlefield. You start with the Swordsman, but as you progress through the story, you will unlock the Mage and eventually a third slot.

### Understanding the Class System

1.  **Swordsman:** Your primary melee dealer. Best for single-target damage and "Coup de Grace" finishers like the Inferno Slash.
2.  **Mage:** Unlocked after Chapter 2, Stage 2. Essential for ranged elemental damage and kiting difficult bosses like the Golem.
3.  **The Tree (Customization):** Reaching Adventure Rank 8 unlocks the skill tree, allowing for deep passive ability customization and class specialization.

The "Coup de Grace" is your ultimate ability, located in the bottom right of the screen. While you can trigger this manually for maximum impact, the game's auto-play feature is surprisingly competent at timing these hits to clear large waves.

## Essential Settings for Performance

To truly enjoy the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow release**, you must dive into the settings menu. By default, the game may not be running at its full potential, and certain visual effects can actually hinder your ability to dodge projectiles.

### Recommended Settings Checklist

| Setting | Recommended Value | Impact on Gameplay |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Frame Rate** | **60 FPS** | Essential for smooth dodging and reactive combat. |
| **Performance Mode** | **ON** | Makes projectiles semi-transparent, making it easier to see your hero. |
| **Ultimate Animation** | **OFF** | Stops the camera zoom during specials, keeping the game flow fast. |
| **Vibrations** | **OFF** | Saves battery life during long grinding sessions. |
| **Graphics Quality** | **High** | Recommended if your device can handle it without thermal throttling. |

> **💡 Tip:** Turning on Performance Mode is the single best thing you can do for high-level play. When the screen is filled with dozens of enemy spells, the semi-transparent effects allow you to pinpoint your character's hitbox much more accurately.

## Progression and AFK Rewards

The **Dragon Quest Smash Grow release** respects your time by including an AFK (Away From Keyboard) system known as the "Search Area." Here, you can deploy small robots on journeys that last up to 12 hours. These robots collect gold, materials, and "Memories"—special equippable items that provide stat boosts to your heroes.

### The Memories System
Memories act as your primary gear progression outside of weapons. They are small creatures or icons that you slot into your characters.
*   **Set Bonuses:** Equipping Memories in specific orders or matching types can grant bonus stats.
*   **Upgrading:** Use materials found in the Training Area (dungeon runs) to rank up your Memories.
*   **Search Priority:** Always keep your robots deployed. The resources they bring back are vital for upgrading the Forge and rank-up areas.

## Advanced Tactics: Mastering the Gauntlet

Once you have secured your Platinum Plums and optimized your settings, your focus should shift to the Gauntlet. Unlike the story mode, which has you moving from area to area, Gauntlets are arena-based survival challenges. You are trapped in a confined space with a timer, and your goal is to achieve the highest score possible by defeating waves of enemies.

The key to the Gauntlet is the "Mage-Swap" tactic. When a boss like the Golem uses a PBAOE (Point Blank Area of Effect) attack, swap to your Mage to continue dealing damage from a distance. Once the boss's high-damage phase ends, swap back to the Swordsman to deliver a high-damage Inferno Slash. This rotation ensures you are never wasting time running away without contributing DPS.
