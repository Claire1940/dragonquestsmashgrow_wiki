## Gameplay Mechanics: Survival and Swapping

The core gameplay loop involves surviving waves of enemies within specific time limits or reaching the end of a story-driven stage. Unlike many other games in the genre, Dragon Quest Smash Grow allows you to field a team of three characters simultaneously, though only one is active at a time.

### Character Classes and Unlocks
You begin your journey with the **Swordsman**, a balanced melee fighter. As you progress through the story chapters, you will unlock additional roles:
1. **Mage**: Unlocked after Chapter 1. Specializes in ranged elemental attacks and AOE spells.
2. **Third Slot**: Unlocked after clearing Chapter 2, Stage 2. This allows for a full three-person rotation.

### The Auto-Play Advantage
The AI in this title is surprisingly competent. When set to auto, the computer will move your character according to their class logic. For example, a Mage will actively try to maintain distance from enemies, while a Swordsman will engage more aggressively. 

> **💡 Tip:** Use the auto-play feature for movement, but manually trigger the **Coup de Grace** (Ultimate) abilities for maximum impact during boss phases.

## Optimization and Settings for Peak Performance

Because the screen can quickly become cluttered with hundreds of monsters and projectiles, adjusting your settings is mandatory for a smooth experience. The UI has been noted by many as being a bit "busy," but the internal system settings offer several ways to clean up the visual noise.

### Recommended System Settings
Navigate to the settings icon in the top right corner and apply the following changes:
*   **Performance Mode**: **ON**. This makes certain particle effects semi-transparent, allowing you to actually see your character amidst the chaos.
*   **Ultimate Animations**: **OFF**. Turning these off prevents the game from pausing every time a special move is used, significantly speeding up your farming runs.
*   **Frame Rate**: Set to **60 FPS** if your device supports it for smoother dodging.
*   **Vibration**: **OFF** to save battery life during long AFK sessions.

## Progression Systems: The Tree and Memories

Progression in Dragon Quest Smash Grow isn't just about leveling up your character; it’s about managing the "Memories" and the "Tree" system. Memories are small creatures or cards that you equip to your heroes to provide passive stat boosts.

### The Search Area (AFK Rewards)
The "Search Area" is the game's idle mechanic. You can send small robots on journeys that last up to 12 hours. These robots return with gold, upgrade materials, and occasionally rare Memories. Prioritizing the upgrades for these robots is essential for players who cannot stay logged in throughout the day.

### The Tree System
Upon reaching **Adventure Rank 8**, you unlock "The Tree." This is a massive talent network that allows you to customize your classes further. You can invest points into passive abilities that increase your health, critical hit rate, or even unlock new class-specific traits. This system ensures that even if two players use the same character, their builds can vary wildly based on their Tree investment.
