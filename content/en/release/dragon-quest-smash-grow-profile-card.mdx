## Gear Progression and Weapon Upgrades

Your performance in the roguelike stages is heavily dependent on your equipment. The game uses a gacha-style progression where you collect weapons and armor, then enhance them using specialized materials like weapon ore and armor ore. These upgrades are reflected on your **dragon quest smash grow profile card**, signaling to the world that you are ready for the game's more difficult challenges.

To upgrade effectively, follow these steps:
1. **Collect Ore:** Earned through main stages and daily training quests.
2. **Use Tablets:** Specific tablets can provide a massive experience boost to a piece of gear.
3. **Ascend Gear:** Once a weapon reaches its level cap, use duplicate items to "break" the limit.
4. **Equip Matching Sets:** Some armor pieces provide set bonuses that significantly increase your survivability.

Progression in *Dragon Quest Smash/Grow* is governed by a stamina system. You start with a cap of 150 points, though the game frequently rewards you with "overflow" stamina that can push you well over 200. Understanding the timing of your stamina recovery is vital for maximizing your daily gains and filling out your **dragon quest smash grow profile card** with rare rewards.

| Action | Stamina Cost | Primary Reward |
|---|---|---|

Planning your play sessions around this window ensures you never waste potential experience gains. Training quests, which unlock at level 4, are the most efficient way to spend stamina if you are looking to boost your character's base stats quickly.

## Mastering the Roguelike Combat

While the **dragon quest smash grow profile card** shows your "static" power, the real test is in the roguelike stages. The combat system is deceptively simple: your character attacks automatically when standing still and stops attacking to move. This creates a rhythmic "move and dodge, stop and strike" flow.

During a run, you will collect temporary power-ups. Some of the most effective combinations in 2026 include:
*   **Double Attack:** Striking twice per interval effectively doubles your DPS.
*   **Wind Balls:** Orbs that circle your character, damaging any enemy that gets too close.
*   **Speed Up:** Increasing your movement speed is essential for dodging magic-casting enemies in later levels.

> **Warning:** Do not rely solely on auto-mode for difficult boss fights. The AI is competent for farming, but it often fails to prioritize dodging telegraphic magic attacks that can end a run instantly.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I unlock the second party member for my dragon quest smash grow profile card?**

A: The second party member, typically a Mage-class character, is unlocked automatically after completing the first few stages of the main campaign. Once unlocked, you can set them as your primary avatar on your profile card through the "Party" menu.

**Q: What is the fastest way to level up my enemy medals?**

A: The most efficient method is to farm specific stages that drop the medal you need and use the "Melt" function. Melting duplicates provides a significant experience boost to the base medal, which in turn increases the stats displayed on your profile.

**Q: Can I change the background of my profile card?**

A: Yes, backgrounds are unlocked by completing specific achievements, such as defeating a certain number of enemies or reaching a specific account level. You can change these in the "Profile Customization" sub-menu.

**Q: Does the auto-battle mode use the same amount of stamina?**

A: Yes, auto-battle costs the same amount of stamina as manual play. However, it is recommended for "cleared" stages where you are simply farming for materials, as the AI may not be able to handle the complex dodging required in new, difficult stages.
