## Essential Settings for a Better Experience

The default UI in Dragon Quest Smash Grow can feel cluttered, and the performance may stutter on older devices or certain tablets. To make your **Dragon Quest Smash Grow reroll** and subsequent grinding more efficient, follow these optimization steps:

1.  **Disable Ultimate Animations:** Go to Settings > Battle. Toggle "Ultimate Animation" to **OFF**. This prevents the game from pausing every time you use a "Coup de Grace," significantly speeding up your clear times.
2.  **Performance Mode:** In the System menu, turn on **Performance Mode**. This makes enemy projectiles and certain effects semi-transparent. In high-density stages, this is vital for seeing where your character is standing and dodging incoming attacks.
3.  **Frame Rate:** Ensure your game is set to **60 FPS**. The default often caps lower to save battery, but the responsiveness at 60 FPS is necessary for the rogue-like dodging mechanics.
4.  **Vibration:** Many players find the constant haptic feedback during auto-play distracting. This can be toggled off in the System menu.

> **Tip:** If you are playing on a tablet or emulator and experiencing a "Stuck on Loading" bug, try switching to a mobile device. As of April 2026, some stability issues remain for non-phone platforms.

## Character Progression and Team Synergy

Your **Dragon Quest Smash Grow reroll** weapon is only the first step. The game allows you to field a team of three characters that you can swap between instantly during combat.

*   **The Swordsman:** Your starting character. Balanced and sturdy.
*   **The Mage:** Unlocks after clearing **Chapter 2, Stage 2**. Essential for ranged elemental damage.
*   **The Third Slot:** Continues to unlock as you progress through the main story.

A pro tip for combat is to utilize the **Auto-Play** feature. The AI in this game is surprisingly competent; it will actively kite enemies if you are using a ranged character like the Mage and will move into melee range only when it is safe for the Swordsman. When facing large bosses like the Golem, swap to your Mage to chip away at its health from a distance, then swap to the Swordsman to unleash a high-damage ultimate when the boss is vulnerable.

## Long-Term Growth: Memories and Search AFK

Once you have finished your **Dragon Quest Smash Grow reroll**, you need to focus on the "Memories" system. Memories are small creatures or cards that you equip to your characters to provide passive stat boosts.

### The Forge and Rank-Ups
In the Forge area, you can upgrade your weapons using materials found in the **Training Area** dungeons. It is important to prioritize upgrading your 3-star reroll weapon first, as it will carry you through the first four chapters of the game without much trouble.

### The Search (AFK) System
Dragon Quest Smash Grow includes an AFK journey system called "Search." You can send robots on journeys that last up to **12 hours**.
*   **Priority:** Upgrade your robots as soon as possible to increase the quality of the loot they return.
*   **Rewards:** These journeys provide gold, upgrade materials, and occasionally new Memories. It is the best way to progress when you aren't actively playing.

### The Skill Tree
Upon reaching **Adventure Rank 8**, you unlock the character tree. This allows for deep customization of your classes, providing passive abilities that can change how your skills function. This is where the "Grow" part of the game title really comes into play, allowing you to tailor your Swordsman or Mage to your specific playstyle.
