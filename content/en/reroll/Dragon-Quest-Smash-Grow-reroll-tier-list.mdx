## Essential Settings for New Players

Before you dive into Chapter 2, there are several settings you should adjust to optimize your experience. The UI can be cluttered, and the default graphics settings may not provide the smoothest gameplay for a rogue-like title.

1.  **Performance Mode:** Navigate to the System settings and turn this ON. It makes certain combat effects semi-transparent. This is vital for seeing enemy projectiles when the screen is filled with monsters.
2.  **60 FPS:** Ensure your frame rate is set to 60 FPS for more responsive dodging and movement.
3.  **Ultimate Animations:** Turn these OFF. While the "Coup de Grace" animations look great, they pause the gameplay and can slow down your grinding efficiency significantly.
4.  **Vibration:** Many players find the constant haptic feedback during auto-play distracting; feel free to disable this in the sound/system menu.

## Combat Strategy and Character Swapping

In this game, you aren't limited to just one hero. As you progress through Chapter 2, you will unlock additional character slots, eventually allowing for a party of three. You can swap between these characters instantly during battle.

| Character Type | Best Usage | Recommended Weapon |

| **Mage** | High burst damage, clearing screen-wide waves. | Staff / Wand |
| **Ranged/Specialist** | Safe kiting, boss DPS. | **Platinum Plums** |

A key strategy is to use your ranged damage dealer (like the Mage or a character equipped with the Platinum Plums) when facing bosses like the Golem. These bosses often have powerful area-of-effect attacks centered on themselves. By staying at a distance, you can chip away at their health without risking a game over. If an enemy gets too close, swap to your Swordsman to push them back with high-knockback melee strikes.

## Progression Systems: Memories and the Tree

Once you have followed the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow reroll tier list** and secured your 3-star weapon, you need to focus on long-term growth. The game uses a system called "Memories"—small creatures that act as equipment.

*   **Memories:** These can be equipped to your characters in specific orders to unlock bonus stats. Always check the "Forge" area to upgrade these and increase your total combat power.
*   **The Tree:** Unlocked at Adventure Rank 8, this system allows for deep customization of your classes. You can unlock passive abilities that apply to all characters, making your entire squad more resilient.
*   **AFK Journeys:** Don't forget to visit the Search area. You can send robots on 12-hour journeys to collect gold, resources, and even rare Memories while you are offline. Prioritize upgrading your search robots to increase the quality of the loot they return.

## Advanced Tips for the Gauntlet

The Gauntlet is where the real challenge lies. Unlike the story mode, which is objective-based, the Gauntlet is a survival mode where you must outlast a timer while defeating waves of enemies. 

*   **Auto-Play Utility:** The AI in this game is surprisingly competent. It will automatically kite enemies and use skills based on the character's class. If you find a stage too difficult to manage manually, try letting the Auto-Play handle the movement while you manually trigger the "Coup de Grace" ultimates.
*   **Health Management:** Look for medicine boxes on the battlefield. If your active character is low on health, swap to a secondary character while you navigate toward a healing item.
*   **Resource Prioritization:** In the early game, focus all your gold and upgrade materials on your 3-star reroll weapon. A single level 20 weapon is much more effective than three level 5 weapons spread across your team.
