## How to use this tier list by progression phase

A good **Dragon Quest Smash Grow character tier list** should change with account progress. Early game priorities are not the same as event optimization priorities.

| Phase | Main Goal | Recommended Core | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Early Story** | Clear stages and secure stars | Warrior + Priest + Mage | Survival and consistent clears |
| **Mid Story/Hard Unlock** | Improve clear speed without dying | Warrior + Priest + Mage | Weapon upgrades and role synergy |
| **Event Farming** | Max score and ticket efficiency | Priest + Warrior + flex DPS | Sustain + damage uptime |
| **Resource Grind** | Gold/weapon material stability | Durable trio | Repeatable runs over risky burst |

### Early game (Rank climb and stage stars)

In early chapters, your biggest enemy is inconsistency. The wrong class swap can tank your stats and fail 3-star runs. Keep your strongest role online and avoid spreading upgrades across too many items.

### Mid-game (Hard mode and event prep)

As harder stages appear, priests gain value quickly because runs get longer and mistakes are punished harder. A reliable healer often beats “faster but fragile” setups.

### Event focus (Tickets and score targets)

Event modes reward stable uptime. If your team wipes or loses tempo, your score and drops suffer. Build for completion first, then optimize damage windows.

## Best team comps and role pairings

This section turns the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow character tier list** into practical team templates.

| Team Comp | Strength | Weakness | Who Should Use It |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Warrior + Priest + Mage** | Balanced damage, sustain, control | Slightly slower burst than glass-cannon options | Most players (recommended default) |
| **Warrior + Priest + Martial Artist** | Better chase pressure and mobility | Less ranged control in dense waves | Players who prefer active swapping |
| **Warrior + Mage + Thief** | Fast map movement and pressure | Lower stability, weaker safety net | Advanced players experimenting |
| **Priest + Mage + Martial Artist** | Good ranged/tempo split | Frontline durability can suffer | Players with strong defensive gear |

### Role assignment checklist

1. **Pick your anchor unit** (usually Warrior or Priest).
2. **Assign your wave clearer** (often Mage).
3. **Use the third slot for utility or tempo**, not just raw damage.
4. **Auto-equip, then manually correct** obvious stat mismatches.
5. **Test in one repeatable stage** before committing resources.

> **💡 Tip:** If your progression stalls, don’t reroll your whole setup immediately. First upgrade one core weapon and one core armor piece on your main trio.

## Common mistakes players make with vocation rankings

Even a solid **Dragon Quest Smash Grow character tier list** can be misused. Most ranking problems come from execution errors, not the list itself.

| Mistake | What Happens | Better Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Swapping to a new vocation with no prep | Unit feels weak and dies fast | Pre-level gear and test in easy stages |

| Spreading upgrades across all classes | No unit reaches power spikes | Focus 2–3 core roles first |
| Chasing burst before stability | Inconsistent 3-star clears | Build consistency, then optimize DPS |

### Should Thief and Martial Artist be ignored?

Not necessarily. They are lower priority right now, but they can still work in player-specific setups. If you enjoy those styles, use them as flex options after your core lineup is built.

### Is Mage overrated or underrated?

Mage can feel amazing with decent investment and wave-heavy content. Without proper gear, performance can look average. That’s why Mage lands in **A tier** for most accounts rather than automatic S tier.
