## Understanding the Reroll System in 2026

Dragon Quest Smash Grow offers a player-friendly start by providing two distinct ways to obtain high-tier gear. Unlike many other gacha games where you must delete your account data repeatedly, this title includes a built-in "Infinite Transmuter" for your first three-star weapon. However, for those looking to "min-max" their account, there is a secondary layer to the reroll process involving limited-time banners.

### The Two Main Summoning Banners

| Banner Name | Pull Limit | Target Item | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Metal Equipment Transmuter** | Limited | **Metal Wing Boomerang** | +5% Damage vs. Slimes (XP Farming) |
| **3-Star Weapon Guaranteed** | **Infinite** | **Fire Blade** or **Falcon Blade** | High DPS and powerful Ultimate synergy |

The first step for any serious player is to check the **Metal Equipment Transmuter**. While this banner requires standard currency and has limited pulls, it contains the Metal Wing Boomerang. This weapon is a "must-have" for long-term efficiency because slimes are the primary source of experience points in Dragon Quest Smash Grow. Increasing your damage against them allows you to clear experience stages much faster, leading to a higher level cap earlier than the rest of the player base.

Once you have attempted the Metal banner, you should move to the **3-Star Weapon Guaranteed Transmuter**. This banner is unique because it allows you to reroll the results an infinite number of times until you confirm the weapon you want.

> **💡 Tip:** Do not settle for the first three-star weapon you see. Since the rerolls are infinite, you should aim specifically for the Fire Blade or the Falcon Blade to maximize your combat potential.

## Top Tier Weapon Analysis

To make the most of this **Dragon Quest Smash Grow guide**, you need to understand the internal rankings of the available three-star weapons. Not all weapons are created equal; some rely heavily on RNG (Random Number Generation), while others provide consistent, massive damage boosts.

### 1. Fire Blade (S-Tier)
The **Fire Blade** is widely considered the best weapon in the game as of 2026. While its base damage potency (250%) is lower than the Falcon Blade, its ultimate ability is peerless.
*   **Element:** Fire
*   **Special Effect:** Immunity to Freeze for 15 seconds.
*   **Ultimate:** Launches two powerful fire projectiles whenever an enemy is defeated.
*   **Why it wins:** In stages with high enemy density, the Fire Blade creates a chain reaction of projectiles that clears the screen faster than any other weapon. It is also exceptionally effective against bosses that summon minions.

### 2. Falcon Blade (S-Tier)
If you prefer raw, consistent physical damage over projectile mechanics, the **Falcon Blade** is your best choice.
*   **Element:** Wind
*   **Base Potency:** 350% (The highest in the game).
*   **Ultimate:** Conjures two wind spheres for 10 seconds and grants immunity to movement/attack speed debuffs.
*   **Synergy:** This weapon has incredible synergy with wind-type blessings, which are common and powerful in the early game.

### 3. Shiver Stick (A-Tier)
The **Shiver Stick** is the premier choice for players who prefer a ranged, tactical playstyle.
*   **Element:** Ice
*   **Base Potency:** 225% Magical Damage.
*   **Special Effect:** Chance to Freeze enemies.
*   **Analysis:** Freeze is the strongest crowd-control (CC) mechanic in Dragon Quest Smash Grow. It works on almost every enemy, providing a safety net that other weapons lack. However, its ultimate requires you to take a hit to trigger a counter-attack, which is counter-intuitive for a ranged character.

## Weapons to Avoid During Reroll

While the infinite reroll banner contains several other weapons, they generally fall short due to being too situational or relying on specific "Blessings" that you might not find during a run.

*   **Platinum Plumes:** This dark-element boomerang inflicts "Spell Lock" (silence). While this sounds good on paper, the majority of enemies in the early to mid-game do not cast spells, making the silence effect entirely useless. Its damage is also notably lackluster compared to the blades.
*   **Crystal Claws:** These lightning claws cause enemies to "flinch" on the final hit. The flinch mechanic is highly inconsistent and poorly defined. Furthermore, its lightning arrow ultimate is easily outclassed by the Fire Blade’s projectiles.
*   **Sandstorm Spear:** The spear is perhaps the most frustrating weapon to use. Its ultimate ability only triggers if you possess a "Spike Type Blessing." If RNG does not grant you that specific blessing during your dungeon crawl, your ultimate becomes a dead slot.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Avoid the Sandstorm Spear and Platinum Plumes. Their reliance on specific enemy types or blessing RNG makes them significantly weaker for general progression.

## Advanced Strategies: Blessings and Synergy

In Dragon Quest Smash Grow, weapons do not exist in a vacuum. Your performance is heavily dictated by the **Blessings** you pick up during your adventure. Blessings are temporary buffs found in stages that can drastically alter how your weapon performs.

### Elemental Synergy
When using the **Falcon Blade**, you should prioritize Wind Blessings. These often stack with the weapon's inherent speed buffs, allowing you to reach an "attack speed cap" where your character becomes a whirlwind of destruction. Conversely, the **Queen’s Whip** requires Sword Type Blessings to activate its ultimate, which can be risky if the game decides to offer you Arrow or Spike blessings instead.

### Scaling with Ranged Weapons
The **Shiver Stick** has a unique scaling mechanic. Its ultimate area of effect (AoE) expands if you possess four or more Arrow Type Blessings. This makes it a "scaling" weapon—it starts average but becomes a behemoth by the end of a long dungeon run. If you enjoy the roguelike elements of the game, the Shiver Stick offers the most satisfying progression.

## Summary of the Reroll Process

1.  Open the **Metal Equipment Transmuter** banner first.
2.  Aim for the **Metal Wing Boomerang** to boost slime farming efficiency.
3.  Proceed to the **Infinite 3-Star Transmuter**.
4.  Reroll until you obtain the **Fire Blade** (Best for AoE) or **Falcon Blade** (Best for raw DPS).
5.  If you prefer ranged combat, settle for the **Shiver Stick** (Best CC) or **Queen's Whip** (High Magic DPS).
6.  Ignore the Platinum Plumes, Crystal Claws, and Sandstorm Spear.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my weapon later in Dragon Quest Smash Grow?**

A: Yes, you can swap weapons between missions. However, the resources required to upgrade weapons are scarce in the early game, which is why choosing the right one during the reroll phase is so critical for this **Dragon Quest Smash Grow guide**.

**Q: Is the Fire Blade good for bosses?**

A: Absolutely. While its "on-kill" projectile effect is best for mobs, the raw fire damage and the immunity to freeze make it a top-tier choice for several major boss encounters in the first few chapters.

**Q: How do I unlock the Infinite Reroll banner?**

A: The Infinite Reroll banner (3-Star Weapon Guaranteed Transmuter) is typically unlocked immediately after completing the game's tutorial phase. It does not require any premium currency for the initial choice.

**Q: Should I keep a 2-star weapon if it has better stats?**

A: Generally, no. Three-star weapons have higher level caps and unique Ultimate abilities that 2-star weapons lack. Even if a 2-star weapon has a higher base attack temporarily, the 3-star weapon will quickly surpass it once upgraded.
