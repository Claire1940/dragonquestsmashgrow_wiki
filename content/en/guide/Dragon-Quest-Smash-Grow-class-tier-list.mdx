## S-Tier: The Gold Standard for DPS

The S-Tier is reserved for weapons that offer the highest damage ceilings and the most reliable ultimate abilities. If you are using the infinite reroll banner, these are the primary targets you should aim for to ensure a smooth progression through the story and endgame content.

### Fire Blade (The Ultimate Boss Killer)
The Fire Blade stands at the pinnacle of the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow class tier list** for 2026. While its base physical fire damage of 250% is respectable, it is the ultimate ability that truly sets it apart. Upon defeating an enemy, the Fire Blade launches two powerful fire projectiles. This mechanic creates a massive damage-per-second (DPS) loop that is unmatched by any other weapon currently in the game. It also provides a 15-second immunity to freeze, though this utility is secondary to its sheer offensive power.

### Falcon Blade (Physical Potency King)
For players who prefer raw speed and high base numbers, the Falcon Blade is a top-tier contender. It boasts a staggering 350% potency physical wind damage, the highest base multiplier among the starting three-star weapons. Its ultimate ability conjures two wind-infused spheres for 10 seconds, which synergize perfectly with wind-type blessings. Furthermore, it grants immunity to movement and attack speed debuffs, making it the most mobile "class" option in the game.

> **💡 Tip:** Pair the Falcon Blade with high-tier Wind Blessings to maximize the uptime and damage of the wind spheres generated by your ultimate.

## A-Tier: Versatile Ranged Options

Not every player wants to be in the thick of melee combat. The A-Tier features weapons that provide excellent safety through range while maintaining competitive damage and utility.

### Shiver Stick (The Crowd Control Expert)
The Shiver Stick is the premier choice for players who value safety and tactical positioning. Dealing 225% magical ice damage, it has a built-in chance to freeze enemies. Unlike other status effects like silence or flinch, "Freeze" works on virtually every enemy in the game, providing a reliable way to interrupt dangerous attacks. Its ultimate expands the area of attack if you possess four arrow-type blessings, allowing for significant scaling as you progress through a run.

### Queen's Whip (The Magic Powerhouse)
If you are building a character focused on Magical Might, the Queen's Whip is your best bet. It deals 200% magical light damage and creates a ward that boosts your attack speed for 10 seconds. The main drawback is that its ultimate is tied to sword-type blessings, which can occasionally lead to unfavorable RNG. However, with a base Magical Might stat of 168, it remains a formidable ranged option for clearing waves of enemies.

## The Reroll Strategy: Maximizing Your Start

Dragon Quest Smash Grow is quite generous with its initial summons, providing a "Three-Star Weapon Guaranteed Transmuter" banner that allows for infinite rerolls. This means you can effectively choose your starting "class" from the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow class tier list** without any cost other than time.

### Step-by-Step Reroll Guide
1. **Tutorial Completion**: Play through the initial tutorial until you unlock the Transmuter (Gacha) menu.
2. **Infinite Banner**: Locate the banner that offers infinite rerolls. This is your primary source for your first S-Tier weapon.
3. **Targeting the S-Tier**: We highly recommend stopping only when you see the **Fire Blade** or the **Falcon Blade**.
4. **The "Mid-Max" Strategy**: If you want to go beyond the basics, look at the "Metal Equipment Transmuter" banner. This banner is separate from the infinite one and requires currency, but it contains the **Metal Wing Boomerang**.

### Why the Metal Wing Boomerang Matters
While it sits in the B-Tier for general combat, the Metal Wing Boomerang is an essential utility tool. It provides a +5% damage bonus against slimes. In the world of Dragon Quest, slimes (especially Metal Slimes) are the primary source of experience points. Having this weapon in your inventory allows you to farm experience stages much more efficiently, significantly speeding up your character's level progression.

## Low Tier Analysis: Weapons to Avoid

Some weapons in the current pool suffer from being too specific or relying on unreliable RNG mechanics. While they may receive buffs in future 2026 updates, they are currently considered "trap" pulls for new players.

*   **Platinum Plumes**: The silence effect is extremely niche. Many enemies do not cast spells, making the primary mechanic of this weapon useless in a majority of encounters.
*   **Crystal Claws**: The "flinch" mechanic is too random and lacks the consistency of the Shiver Stick’s freeze.
*   **Sandstorm Spear**: This weapon requires "Spike-type" blessings to trigger its ultimate. These blessings are relatively rare, often leaving you with a dead ultimate ability during critical moments.

## FAQ

**Q: Which weapon is the absolute best for a new player?**

A: According to the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow class tier list**, the Fire Blade is the best overall choice. Its ultimate ability provides the highest damage output in the game, making it exceptionally effective against bosses.

**Q: How do I get the infinite reroll?**

A: The game features a specific "Three-Star Weapon Guaranteed Transmuter" banner available at the start. You can refresh the results of this banner as many times as you like until you get the specific weapon you want.

**Q: Is the Metal Wing Boomerang worth spending gems on?**

A: If you are looking to "min-max" your experience gain, yes. The 5% damage bonus against slimes is invaluable for farming experience in the long run, though it is not as strong for clearing difficult boss stages as the Fire Blade.

**Q: Do weapons represent different classes?**

A: Yes, in Dragon Quest Smash Grow, your weapon determines your playstyle (melee vs. ranged), your elemental affinity, and your ultimate ability, effectively acting as your character class.

**Q: What is the benefit of the Shiver Stick over the Queen's Whip?**

A: The Shiver Stick offers superior crowd control through its freeze mechanic, which works on almost all enemies. The Queen's Whip offers higher raw magic damage but lacks the defensive utility of the freeze effect.
