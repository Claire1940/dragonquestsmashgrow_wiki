## Weapon Tier List and Rankings

While the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow fire blade** is the undisputed king of the reroll, other weapons serve specific niches that might appeal to different playstyles. If you prefer a more defensive approach or a magic-focused build, you might consider the following alternatives:

### 1. Falcon Blade (The Speed King)
The Falcon Blade is the second-best choice for physical attackers. It boasts a base power of approximately 350 and features a unique passive that can freeze enemies. This is ideal for players who want to avoid taking damage entirely, as a frozen enemy cannot retaliate. However, be aware that it may slightly reduce your own movement speed in exchange for this massive utility.

### 2. Silver Stick (The Mage's Choice)
Essentially an ice-based staff, the Silver Stick is the premier ranged weapon. If you intend to play as a Mage, this is your primary target. It allows you to kite enemies from a distance, keeping you safe while dealing consistent elemental damage.

### 3. Metal Wing Boomerang (The Farming Tool)
This weapon is usually found in the "Metal Equipment" banner rather than the standard reroll. It is the absolute best tool for leveling up because it provides a 5% damage bonus specifically against Slimes (Limus). In the world of Dragon Quest, hunting metal slimes is the fastest way to gain experience, making this boomerang an essential secondary item.

## Vocations and Class Synergy

In Dragon Quest Smash Grow, your character starts as a base unit, but you eventually unlock "Vocations" (classes) like Warrior, Mage, Cleric, and Gladiator. To unlock these, you must progress through the story—specifically completing Chapter 1, Stage 5, and reaching Chapter 2, Stage 2.

Your weapon choice during the reroll phase should dictate which vocation you pursue. If you successfully rolled the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow fire blade**, you are perfectly positioned to become a Gladiator. This class excels at physical combat and leverages the Fire Blade's passive to its maximum potential.

Conversely, if you chose the Silver Stick, you must transition into the Mage class. Combining the Silver Stick with a blessing like "Blazing of Eternal Blaze" creates a "broken" build that allows you to spam high-tier fire spells without cooldown penalties.

> **Warning:** Switching to a vocation that does not match your primary weapon's stat focus (e.g., using a Silver Stick on a Warrior) will significantly gimp your damage output in later chapters.

**Q: Is the Falcon Blade better than the Fire Blade for bosses?**

A: The Falcon Blade is excellent for bosses that move quickly because of its freeze chance. However, the **Dragon Quest Smash Grow fire blade** usually wins out in overall DPS because its passive triggers more frequently against both bosses and mobs.

**Q: What is the best vocation for a f2p player?**
